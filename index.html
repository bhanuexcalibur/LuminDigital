01The Narrative

Solaire is proof the flywheel already turns. Right now it reads like a separate AI announcement.

Lumin has the receipts: near 90 NPS, zero client churn, an independent study proving value compounds instead of flattening out. Solaire should be the clearest evidence of that thesis. Three surfaces currently undersell it.

Surface 01 · lumindigital.com homepage
Current
A capability list, retail banking, commercial banking, AI, weekly updates, then usage stats underneath. Reads like an inventory, not a reason to care.
I’d replace it with
The 451 Research finding itself, stated as the headline: Lumin is the platform proven to get more valuable every year, not less. Not a tagline. The study already sits on this site.
Why: the homepage buries its strongest asset under a feature list any competitor could also claim.
Surface 02 · Lumin Solaire launch language
Current
Leads with architecture: “built from the ground up, not retrofitted,” on a “unified data foundation.” Right for a CTO. Not what a credit union president repeats to their board.
I’d replace it with
One buyer-facing line, right after the architecture claim: “The model doesn’t reset every time you add a feature.” That’s what “not retrofitted” means to whoever signs the contract.
Why: the architecture claim is real. It just stops one sentence short of the board.
Surface 03 · Solaire Assist, December 2025
Current
Target Manager Assistant, Secure Form Assistant, FAQ Assistant, framed as time-savers. Accurate, filed under operational efficiency.
I’d replace it with
A re-launch as proof, not a feature update: the first live evidence the compounding-growth architecture already runs in production, months before conversational AI even ships.
Why: Lumin’s strongest answer to “every vendor says they’re doing AI” is currently marketed like a minor release.
02Buyer Personas

One deal, three very different questions about the same platform

FIs buy Lumin as a committee, not a person. Here are the three seats at that table, based on roles across Lumin’s own case studies, and what each one needs to hear.

What they ask
“We’re a $3.5B community bank competing with names members already trust. What does this get us that a bigger, cheaper name doesn’t?”
What worries them
Every RFP demo, Alkami included, promises modern and AI-enabled. They need one number that isn’t marketing.
I’d lead with
The one stat that reframes the deal: 75% of platform value comes from revenue growth, not cost reduction. That moves the conversation from cheapest vendor to platform that grows with them, which is where Lumin wins.
What they ask
“What happens to my core integration and my uptime once Solaire layers on top?”
What worries them
They’ve lived through a legacy vendor’s overnight-outage upgrade. “AI-native” can sound like a rip-and-replace they’d have to explain to the board.
I’d lead with
The uptime number, paired with a peer reference already on Lumin’s site: a CTO calling their conversion one of the smoothest their credit union has run. Solaire is a layer on trusted infrastructure, not a new migration.
What they ask
“Will members actually notice this, or is it another back-office tool nobody sees?”
What worries them
A chatbot that gives a wrong answer becomes their support-queue problem, not IT’s.
I’d lead with
FAQ Assistant learns from that FI’s own Secure Message volume and search behavior, not a generic knowledge base. That’s “the platform got better at knowing our members,” not “we added a chatbot.”

04Sales Enablement

A battlecard for the objection reps are already hearing

Every core processor now ships some AI assistant. That’s probably the most common objection reps hear on a Solaire call. Here’s how I’d equip them to answer it.

When the prospect says: “Our core already shipped an AI assistant. Why do we need Lumin’s?”
I’d train reps to ask one question before comparing chatbots: “Does that assistant see what your marketing tools and member service queue see, or only what’s inside the core?” Most core-native AI only sees balances and transactions, not app searches, campaign clicks, or last week’s support chat.

Solaire runs on one data foundation across the whole platform. That’s why FAQ Assistant can pull from Secure Message topics and in-app search in the same breath, not two systems sharing a login screen.
Then send: Solaire Assist’s production case examples, not the roadmap deck. Shipped and in daily use, not a promise.
Why it works: arguing whose AI is smarter is a demo-off Lumin doesn’t need to win. The data-unification question is one a prospect’s own IT team wants answered anyway, and it’s where a bolt-on assistant structurally can’t compete.
